operators

= searches just the words themselves. there are a couple fancy things you can do with this:

* matches anything (or nothing, i.e. it's /.*/)
? matches any single letter, /[aeıoumpbfntdczsrljꝡqkg'h]|[ncs]h/i
C, V, F, Q match their refgram definitions (note that VV ⊋ F)
i = ı
v = w = y = ꝡ
- or . or ◌̣ mark the end of prefixes
1234 also work for ◌̀◌́◌̈◌̂
R matches a raku
_ matches a space
()[]| work like in regex

e.g. you can find conjunctions prefixed with na- using =na.R*2. per consensus rie2i = ríe'ı

~ is similar but will also match word-internally: ~◌ = =*◌*

@ searches word authors

# find the word with that id

/ finds definitions with a certain number of slots

$ searches just the scores. you can do e.g. $<-2 to find spam (why would you want to do that though)

! or - negate a query, e.g. -@official
